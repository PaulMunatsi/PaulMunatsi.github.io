Who We Are

Pawafan - Building Zimbabwe's Sustainable Energy Future, One Community at a Time

Pawafan is a dynamic, for-profit social enterprise founded by a new generation of Zimbabwean leaders. We tackle the critical issue of energy poverty head-on by delivering smart, scalable, and sustainable energy innovations to the communities that need them most.

The Problem We're Solving

The Energy Access Crisis in Zimbabwe

üéØ The Core Problem

Rural Zimbabwe faces a severe energy access gap, with a vast majority of households and small-scale enterprises lacking a connection to the national grid or any reliable, affordable, and modern source of electricity. This energy poverty is a fundamental bottleneck to achieving sustainable development.

‚ö†Ô∏è The Primary Manifestations & Consequences
SDG 3 & 5 The Health and Gender Crisis

Reliance on solid fuels (firewood) for cooking in poorly ventilated homes causes debilitating indoor air pollution, a leading cause of pneumonia and other respiratory diseases. Women and children, who spend the most time near the hearth, bear the greatest health burden.

SDG 4 The Educational Deficit

Lack of lighting prevents children from studying after dark, contributing to high dropout rates and poor educational outcomes. The digital divide widens as phones and radios‚Äîcritical tools for information‚Äîcannot be charged, limiting access to knowledge.

SDG 8 The Economic Paralysis

For smallholder farmers and micro-entrepreneurs, the absence of power is a direct barrier to prosperity. This manifests in two critical ways:

  • Limited Productive Use of Energy: Inability to use basic machinery (e.g., sewing machines, barber clippers, water pumps) to create goods or services beyond daylight hours, stifling micro-enterprise.
  • Blocked Commercial Use of Energy: Inability to scale operations with vital agro-processing equipment (e.g., grinders, milk chillers, irrigation systems). This locks farmers into low-profit, rain-fed subsistence agriculture and leads to significant post-harvest losses.
SDG 7 Lack of Affordable & Clean Energy

The current energy mix for rural communities is defined by a lack of affordability, reliability, and cleanliness, creating a direct contradiction to the targets of SDG 7.

SDG 13 The Unsustainable Pathway

Dependence on deforestation for firewood contributes to environmental degradation and undermines climate resilience, trapping communities in a vulnerable state.

Commercial & Agri-Solar: Fueling Rural Enterprises

Pawafan power your business and increase productivity with reliable energy that is independent of the grid.

  • Solar Water Pumping: Enable all-season irrigation to boost crop yields and diversify farming.
  • Agri-Processing Power: Run grain mills, dehullers, and grinders to add value to your produce
  • Cold Storage Solutions: Drastically reduce post-harvest losses with solar-powered refrigerators and cold rooms for milk, fruits, and vegetables.
  • Custom Commercial Systems: We design and install larger systems to meet the specific power needs of shops, clinics, and small factories.

Biogas Systems: Clean Cooking, Productive Farming

Transform animal and plant waste into a powerful source of clean energy and organic fertilizer.

  • Clean & Safe Cooking: Replace smoke-filled kitchens with a healthy, continuous gas supply for faster cooking.
  • Waste-to-Value: Convert livestock manure into free cooking fuel and nutrient-rich, organic fertilizer.
  • Reduce Costs: Eliminate the need to buy firewood, charcoal, or chemical fertilizers, saving families money and time.
