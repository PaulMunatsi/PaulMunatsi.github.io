About Pawafan

Who We Are

Pawafan is a for-profit youth-led social enterprise founded in 2023 that provides smart energy innovations and clean cooking solutions for low-income households and smallholder farmers. Our work focuses on providing sustainable energy solutions, particularly benefiting women and small-scale farmers in rural Zimbabwe.

The Problem We're Solving

In Zimbabwe, 70% of the population (approximately 10 million people) lacks access to reliable electricity, with 75% living in rural areas. 90% of rural households rely on firewood and biomass for cooking, resulting in over 4,000 deaths annually from indoor air pollution-related diseases. Energy poverty costs Zimbabwe's economy $1.2 billion annually, hindering economic development and perpetuating poverty.

Our Target Market

Pawafan is targeting 1.5 million households in Zimbabwe, representing approximately 7.5 million people. Our focus is on low-income households with monthly income less than $100, living in rural and peri-urban areas without access to grid electricity.

We aim to capture 5% of this market, equivalent to 75,000 households in the next 5 years, with an average revenue per user of $15 to $20 per month.
