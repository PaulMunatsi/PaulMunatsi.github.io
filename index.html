About Me

As a Digital Security Analyst at Digital Society of Africa, I architect and deploy secure digital infrastructure for 70+ mission-driven organizations across Sub-Saharan Africa. My work bridges the gap between cutting edge security practices and the practical realities of civil society designing resilient, privacy respecting systems for human rights defenders, journalists, and grassroots movements navigating hostile digital environments.

I specialize in holistic security approaches that blend technical rigor with human-centered design. From conducting comprehensive risk assessments and implementing ISO 27001-aligned governance frameworks to deploying encrypted cloud platforms and mentoring frontline digital security teams, I ensure organizations can operate safely without compromising their mission. Over five years, I've completed 40+ ICT audits and delivered transformative training that bridges diverse digital literacy levels and drives lasting adoption of secure practices.

Technology, in my view, should be a force multiplier for justice, not a barrier. Every solution I design prioritizes accessibility, sustainability, and the unique contextual needs of communities working to create positive change. Whether building mesh networks for offline resilience or crafting incident response protocols for at-risk defenders, I bring both technical precision and a deep commitment to the movements I serve.
